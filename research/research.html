Urban Building Window Detection

2022 - present // Environmental Systems Lab, Cornell University
Project Lead // Team Member: Ann Ren, Curtis Xu, Tony Liu (former) // Advisor: Timur Dogan

Project Details

We present a scalable and automated method for façade reconstruction and window detection using Google Photorealistic 3D Tiles, for high LOD UBEM. Our pipeline begins by extracting individual façade texture maps from Google 3D Tiles through the development of advanced geometric processing algorithms. These texture maps are then processed using state-of-the-art semantic segmentation models trained to identify windows on façades. To further enhance accuracy, a post-segmentation optimization step incorporates pattern recognition and large-vision models to refine the segmentation results. This workflow is designed to be robust against the noise and blurriness inherent in large-scale photorealistic data, offering a reliable and scalable approach to building geometry reconstruction for UBEM applications.

Urban Building Information Preprocessing

2023 - present // Environmental Systems Lab, Cornell University
Individual Project // Advisor: Timur Dogan

Project Details

I have been actively contributing to the Environmental Systems Lab’s development of Urban Building Energy Modeling (UBEM) tools to support Ithaca, NY’s Green New Deal goal of achieving net-zero emissions by 2030. My work focuses on developing a building energy model archetype extraction package to synthesize key information on construction, internal loads, and systems from EnergyPlus input files. Additionally, I have been creating a data processing framework in to handle and standardize diverse urban datasets for simulation. Furthermore, I have played a key role in designing and implementing core geometrical data structures and infrastructure to enable high Level of Detail (LOD) building energy simulations.

Thermal Comfort Analysis

2023 - present // KPF
Team Member: Amber Su, Remy Mermelstein, Christina Brown // Advisor: Carlos Cerezo Davila

Project Details

This research focuses on three key aspects: developing custom scripts to integrate hourly simulated CFD wind from prevailing wind directions into outdoor thermal comfort analysis, incorporating simulated shortwave radiation into indoor thermal comfort calculations to better reflect localized radiative conditions compared to the uniform diffuse and reflective assumptions in ASHRAE, and creating a dynamic thermal sensation model to assess occupants' comfort under transient conditions.

Currently, we are working on expanding the research to explore the applications of this analysis in design practice and explore the potential of other multi-node thermoregulatory heat balance and transient thermal comfort model.

Building Energy Modeling Automation and Visualization

2024 // KPF
Advisor: Carlos Cerezo Davila

Project Details

I developed and optimized workflows in Grasshopper to automate early-stage energy modeling, focusing on building geometry processing, including thermal zone separation, window and shading generation. Additionally, I defined space loads, created construction templates, and established a visualization pipeline.
